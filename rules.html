The Basics

The goal is to have a hand total closer to 21 than the dealer's hand, without going over 21 (busting).

  • **Card Values:** Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value. Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10 points. Aces are worth 1 or 11 points, whichever is better for the hand.
  • **Blackjack:** A hand totaling exactly 21 with the first two cards (an Ace and any 10-point card) is a natural Blackjack.
  • **Dealer's Turn:** The dealer must hit on all hands totaling 16 or less and must stand on all hands totaling 17 or more.

Player decisions

After receiving your initial two cards, you have the following options:

  • **Hit:** Take an additional card to increase your hand total.
  • **Stand:** Keep your current hand and end your turn.
  • **Double Down:** Double your initial bet and receive exactly one more card, then your turn ends. This is usually permitted only on the first two cards.

Insurance

If the dealer's up-card is an Ace, you may place an Insurance bet, which is half your original bet.

  • This is a bet that the dealer has a Blackjack (a 10-point card as their down-card).
  • If the dealer has Blackjack, the Insurance bet pays 2:1. If they do not, you lose the Insurance bet.

Bets and Returns

Winnings are paid based on the outcome of the hand:

  • **Win (Standard):** If your hand is higher than the dealer's without busting, you are paid 1:1 (even money).
  • **Blackjack:** A natural Blackjack pays 3:2 (you win $3 for every $2 bet).
  • **Push (Tie):** If your hand total equals the dealer's total, it is a push, and your bet is returned.
  • **Loss:** If your hand is lower than the dealer's or you bust, you lose your bet.
